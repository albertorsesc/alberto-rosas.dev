# Personal Branding Research — alberto-rosas.dev Overhaul

## Executive Summary

The era of the portfolio-as-resume is over. Outstanding personal sites in 2025-2026 are **owned corners of the internet** where the human comes first and the credentials come second. For Alberto — AI Engineer, Harley rider, father, Mexican, 12-year veteran who pivoted into AI — there is a rich, differentiated identity on the table. The current site uses about 10% of it.

---

## 1. Content Strategy

### The Three Questions Every Homepage Must Answer Instantly
1. Who are you as a human being?
2. What do you do professionally?
3. Why does that combination matter?

The current intro ("I architect Intelligent Systems and AI Agents") answers #2 but skips #1 entirely.

### High-Value Pages to Add

#### `/now` Page
Derek Sivers popularized this (nownownow.com). Answers: "What would you tell a friend you hadn't seen in a year?" Over 2,300 developers maintain one. Alberto's `/now` could include: current AI project, a book he's reading, something the family is doing, a route he's riding.

#### `/uses` Page
Popularized by Wes Bos. Lists tools, hardware, software, setup. Highly searched, extremely personality-revealing. For an AI engineer: dev environment, preferred models, AI tools he trusts, desk setup — and whether the Harley has a dashcam.

#### The Origin Story (Properly Told)
The pivot from web engineer to AI architect is compelling. The "from Mexico" context is a differentiator. Three-act structure: Traditional Web Engineering → Industry Shift → AI Architect.

#### "Work vs. Play" Separation
Gloria Lo's site separates professional work from personal creative pursuits with equal design care. Alberto could do the same: engineering in one section, personal projects/side experiments in another.

#### Personal Interests / Life Page
Not a bullet list — a real window into life outside the terminal. The Harley, family, and Mexican identity are three distinct personality anchors.

## 3. Visual Identity

### Photography Over Avatars
- One strong, slightly casual professional shot (not corporate headshot)
- A candid shot on/near the Harley
- A family photo (human, not staged)
- An "at work" shot — terminal, coffee, natural light
- **The GitHub avatar is the single highest-leverage item to replace**

### Color: Cyan Works, But Needs a Warm Counterpoint
- Keep cyan as professional/tech accent
- Introduce warm accent (amber/rust) for personal sections
- Warm tones resonate with Mexico's visual culture (terracotta, amber, ochre)
- Two colors telling two true stories

### Typography as Personality
- **Sans-serif** (current: Inter) = modern, technical → keep for body
- **Serif headline accent** = authority, depth, craft → introduce for hero/section headings
- Pairing: strong serif (Playfair Display, Fraunces, Source Serif) for large text + clean sans for everything else
- Says "I have depth AND I'm modern"

### Custom Visual Touches
- Single custom illustration (silhouette with Harley? at desk with terminal?)
- Hand-drawn texture accents or border treatments
- Random hover color shifts (Cassidy Williams pattern)

## 4. Interaction Design for Personal Feel

### Hover States & Cursor Effects
- Nav hover color burst per item
- Project card subtle 3D tilt (CSS perspective + rotateX/Y)
- "Currently building" live section that feels alive
- Extended scroll-triggered reveals with varied timing

### Custom 404 Page
"Looks like this road ends here. Even on the best routes, you hit a dead end sometimes."
[Take me home →]
Or: photo of bike parked at end of a road.

### Micro-Copy Replacements

| Generic | Personal |
|---|---|
| "Contact Me" | "Let's Build Something" |
| "Download CV" | "Grab My Resume" |
| "Page not found" | "This road's a dead end. Head back." |
| Blog section header | "Things I've Been Thinking About" |
| Skills section | "What I Build With" |
| Footer email | "Drop me a line" |

### Easter Eggs
- **Console message**: Styled `console.log` when devtools open — "Hey, you opened devtools. I respect that."
- **Konami code**: ↑↑↓↓←→←→BA triggers Harley rev sound or surprise
- **Time-based greeting**: "Buenos días" / "Buenas tardes" / "Buenas noches" — a Mexico nod developers will remember
